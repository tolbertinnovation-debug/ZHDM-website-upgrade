Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our mission, ministry, and community impact in Liberia.

General Information

What is ZHDMI?
ZHDMI stands for Zion Healing and Deliverance Ministries International. We are a Christian-based, non-profit NGO registered in the Republic of Liberia. Our dual focus is on spiritual restoration and practical community development.
Where are you located?
Our principal office is located in the Johnsonville Community, Montserrado County, Liberia. We also operate outreach centers and prayer hubs across the region.
Are you a church or an NGO?
We are both. While we operate prayer centers and hold weekly Sunday worship services, we are formally registered as an NGO to carry out humanitarian work in education, health awareness, and poverty reduction.

Our Programs

What kind of humanitarian work do you do?
We run six core programs including Education Support (scholarships), Health Awareness (community drives), Youth & Women Empowerment, and Discipleship. Our goal is to meet the physical and spiritual needs of Liberian families.
Who is eligible for your education scholarships?
We prioritize underserved children and youth within the Montserrado County area who demonstrate academic potential but lack the financial means to cover school fees and supplies.
How can I join a prayer vigil?
Our All-Night Prayer and Deliverance Vigils are held monthly at our Johnsonville center. All are welcome! Check our News & Events page for the next scheduled date.

Giving & Transparency

Is ZHDMI a registered non-profit?
Yes, we are a fully registered non-profit NGO in the Republic of Liberia. We operate under strict governance and accountability standards set by our Board of Directors.
How are my donations used?
100% of public donations go directly toward our mission programs. We maintain radical transparency and publish Annual Reports detailing our fund allocation and community outcomes.
Can I volunteer even if I am not in Liberia?
Absolutely. We offer virtual volunteer roles in administration, media, and prayer coordination. For those in Liberia, we have many hands-on roles in our community programs.
